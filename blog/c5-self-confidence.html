Self-Confidence

8 May 2016

What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

Mindfulness is a state of active, open attention on the present. When you're mindful, you observe your thoughts and feelings from a distance, without judging them good or bad. Instead of letting your life pass you by, mindfulness means living in the moment and awakening to experience. Focusing on being mindful can lead to greater self awareness, the benefits of which include:

  • being fully present, here and now,
  • becoming aware of what you’re avoiding,
  • becoming more connected to yourself, to others and to the world around you,
  • becoming less reactive to unpleasant experiences, and
  • having more direct contact with the world, rather than living through your thoughts.

My body scan experience

I have practiced the body scan technique quite frequently in the past. I used to do regular yoga classes that at the end incorporated a savasana (corpse) pose including the body scan process. I found the process very relaxing and useful for bringing my attention to particular parts of my body that I may usually ignore.

What did you take away from the 'My Emotions are Not Me' section?

I enjoyed this section. I think the shift in thinking from ‘I am this emotion’ to ‘I feel this emotion’ could be powerful for controlling my feelings and ties in especially well with the mindfulness and self-awareness concepts discussed above.

The ‘clouds passing in the sky’ analogy is interesting and I hope to remember it when I look at the clouds, which is semi-frequently.
