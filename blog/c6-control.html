Control

15 May 2016

What did I take away from chapter 5 of ‘Search Inside Yourself’?

An important concept introduced this week was controlling our emotions instead of letting them control us. In particular, we are encouraged to identify our triggers when they occur. I enjoyed the ‘Siberian North Railroad’ mnemonic and think it will be useful:

  • Stop – Stop or pause when you feel you feel a negative reflex response.
  • Breathe – Breathing helps calm us.
  • Notice – Take time to notice your physical state of being as well as your emotions.
  • Reflect – Consider why you responded in the way you did once the moment has passed.
  • Respond – Respond in a positive and kind manner.

My two minutes of mindfulness

I initially attempted the 'easy' (i.e, focus on your breathing) two minutes of mindfulness. I found it to be an interesting experience.

I set a timer for two minutes and shut my eyes. My mind did occasionally wander (e.g, what am I going to write in my blog?) but I managed to bring it back to focusing on my breathing. I think for a small period I even managed to think about.. nothing. The two minutes passed faster than I expected.
