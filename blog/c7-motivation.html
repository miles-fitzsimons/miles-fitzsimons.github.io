How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Pleasure, passion, and purpose are three types of happiness that vary in sustainability. Pleasure is the easiest of the three to attain, and the one that most people think will bring them happiness in life. But this is also the most short-lived form of happiness, which causes people to then have to find the next new thing to provide them pleasure.

Passion is harder to come by, but if found, can bring someone a more sustainable sense of happiness. Finally, purpose is the most sustainable form of happiness and comes from taking part in something bigger than yourself, creating good in something that may not, and probably doesn't, revolve around you.

How did you find the resilience ideas?

I believe in the concept of resiliency and have come across it many times in both my personal and professional life. The ideas were very effective. Having a calm body and mind (inner calm) led to emotional stability during the exercise. Cognitive resilience is being optimistic tied with the ability to take an objective perspective of your successes and failures. A large part of building resiliency comes from being mindful. If we are aware of our emotions, antecedents and get perspective on everything that is happening, often we can take actions to protect ourselves.
